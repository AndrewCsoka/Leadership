# Report writing

- **Subject: if they read nothing else, this should be enough.** It should convey the status and what action they need to take. Tailor it to your audience: a CTO needs impact and escalation ("Project is amber - needs more resource"), a project manager needs progress and decisions ("Project is amber - backend delayed, need decision on scope by Wednesday")
- **Use plain language.** Say it directly. "We haven't decided yet" not "we are currently in the process of evaluating options"
- **Only include detail that explains a blocker or problem.** The reader doesn't need to know how you got here, only where things are stuck and why
- **Use a consistent layout each time.** Agree the format with your audience and stick to it. Familiarity lets them scan faster because they know where to look
- **Use RAG (Red, Amber, Green) colour statuses.** They are coarse by design, which is a strength: you won't agonise over the difference between 6/10 or 7/10) and percentages create false precision and break when you discover new work. Include the previous status to show direction e.g. "Amber (previously Green)". Direction matters because the same colour means different things depending on where it came from. "Amber (previously Red)" is a recovery while "Amber (previously Green)" is a warning
